  • 4. Linear Regression
    Fitting a straight line through noisy data. Least squares, the slope formula, and $R^2$.
  • 5. Recurring Patterns
    Seasonality in data. How to detect it, measure it, and separate it from the trend.

  • 2. Multiplicative Decomposition

  • 3. Seasonal Index Floor

    Reducing seasonal amplitude for mixed cost buckets using the fixed ratio.
  • 5. Why Not ARIMA?
    The degrees-of-freedom crisis with 12–24 months of data. When simple decomposition is the honest choice.

Phase 5 — Uncertainty Quantification

Every forecast is wrong. The question is how wrong, and whether the uncertainty grows in a principled way.

  • 1. Random Walks and Brownian Motion
    Why forecast errors accumulate as $\sigma\sqrt{t}$, not $\sigma t$.
  • 2. The Uncertainty Model
    The $\sigma(w) = \min(\text{BASE} + k\sqrt{w-1},\;\text{MAX})$ function. Fan charts.
  • 3. Combining Independent Uncertainties
    Quadrature: $\sigma_{\text{total}} = \sqrt{\sigma_1^2 + \sigma_2^2}$. Confidence intervals via z-scores.

Mathematician Biographies

The people behind the mathematics. Short biographies focused on what each person contributed to the tools used in this course.

  • Carl Friedrich Gauss (1777–1855)
    Predicted the orbit of Ceres using least squares. The theoretical foundation of linear regression.
  • Adrien-Marie Legendre (1752–1833)
    Published the method of least squares in 1805. The first clear formulation of the regression recipe.
  • Francis Galton (1822–1911)
    Coined “regression to the mean.” Introduced percentiles and the concept of correlation.
  • Luca Pacioli (1447–1517)
    Codified double-entry bookkeeping. The accounting identity behind three-way forecasting.
  • Robert Brown & Norbert Wiener
    Brownian motion: from pollen grains to the $\sigma\sqrt{t}$ law behind fan charts.
  • Britton, Fisher & Whitley
    Invented the fan chart at the Bank of England. Turning uncertainty into a visual language.
  • Adolphe Quetelet (1796–1874)
    Invented the “average man.” Pioneered applying statistics to social phenomena.
  • Karl Pearson (1857–1936)
    Named “standard deviation,” developed the chi-squared test, founded the first statistics department.
  • John Tukey (1915–2000)
    Invented the box plot and Tukey fences. The father of exploratory data analysis.
  • Jacob Bernoulli (1655–1705)
    Discovered the constant $e$ through compound interest and proved the law of large numbers.
  • Fibonacci (c. 1170–c. 1250)
    Liber Abaci introduced Hindu-Arabic numerals to Europe, motivated by commerce and interest.
  • Charles Horngren (1926–2011)
    Defined the modern framework for CVP analysis and cost behaviour classification.
