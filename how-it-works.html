How the forecast works.

A procedural three-statement model, grounded in standard accounting practice. Built to be explained.

The model

Deterministic code. Standard methodology. No black boxes.

Every figure on every projection is computed by procedural code following established financial-modelling practice. The engine takes your accounting data, derives a set of forecasting assumptions, and projects forward week by week to produce a complete profit and loss, balance sheet and cash flow statement.

The same inputs always produce the same outputs. Each assumption can be traced back to the historical data point or default value it came from.

How AI is used

Language goes in. Numbers come out of code.

A language model translates your natural-language scenarios — “what if I hire two engineers in June and lose my biggest customer in September” — into structured events and assumption changes that the procedural engine then projects.

The language model never produces a number that appears on a financial statement. It writes the request; the deterministic model writes the answer.

Foundations

Built on the same methods accountants already use.

Three-statement integration

P&L flows into the balance sheet and cash flow exactly as required by IAS 7 and FRS 102 Section 7, using the indirect method.

Working capital

DSO, DPO and cash conversion cycle computed and projected per the conventions of the AFP, ACT and CFA Institute credit-analysis curricula.

Cost behaviour

Fixed, variable and residual costs classified using the cost-volume-profit framework taught in the CIMA P2 syllabus and Horngren’s textbook.

Seasonality

Classical multiplicative decomposition (ratio-to-moving-average), the same mathematical core used by the ONS and the US Census Bureau.

Tax timing

VAT, PAYE/NI and corporation tax accruals modelled on the actual HMRC payment cycles, not as instantaneous P&L charges.

Forecast uncertainty

Confidence bands that widen with the forecast horizon, derived from the same square-root-of-time rule used in Bank of England fan charts.

Internal consistency

The model checks itself.

Assets = Liabilities + Equity is enforced at every weekly close. Cash is the residual that makes the balance sheet balance.
Closing cash on the cash flow statement equals cash on the balance sheet, period by period, by construction.
Net profit flows into retained earnings; depreciation reduces fixed assets; working-capital changes reconcile to cash flow movements.

Both balance checks are reported on every output. A breach is a model bug, not a user input issue, and surfaces immediately.

What it isn’t

Honest about scope.

The engine is a tool to produce a defensible projection from a stated set of assumptions. It does not replace the judgement of a qualified accountant on going concern, fair value, deferred tax or impairment. It is not designed for forecasts that go into regulated communications without independent reporting accountant review.

It is at the proof-of-concept stage. The methodology will continue to develop, and feedback from qualified accountants and finance leaders is the principal mechanism by which it sharpens.

A note on accuracy. Every forecast carries uncertainty. The engine’s purpose is not to claim a level of precision it cannot deliver, but to produce a projection whose construction can be explained, defended and reproduced.
