Three-Way Forecasting Mathematics — Course Summary

A self-study course covering every mathematical concept used in a three-way accounting forecast model (P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow). The goal: rebuild the entire model pen-and-paper from memory, defendable to an outside mathematician or accountant. Every formula is derived from first principles, never dropped from the sky.

Phase 0 — The Statistical Toolkit: Mean vs median (order statistics, robustness), variance and standard deviation (Bessel's correction, degrees of freedom), coefficient of variation (dimensionless regularity measure, 0.35 threshold), percentiles and IQR (distribution shape), linear regression (OLS derivation, slope formula, geometric interpretation), recurring pattern detection (median interval, frequency classification, CV-based regularity test, confidence scoring).

Phase 1 — Exponential Growth and the Time Value of Money: Compound interest from repeated multiplication, continuous compounding (1+r)^(t/T), CAGR derivation and clamping to [-20%, +25%]. Geometric series and annuity formula PMT = Pr/(1-(1+r)^(-n)), amortisation schedules, annual-to-weekly rate conversion. CVP cost classification (fixed/variable/semi-variable), inflation compounding, blended growth rate for residual costs, depreciation stripping, frequency normalisation (4.33 weeks/month).

Phase 2 — Seasonal Decomposition: 12-month centred moving average as low-pass filter (half-weights at endpoints, eliminates annual frequency). Multiplicative decomposition x(t) = T(t)×S(t)×I(t), ratio-to-moving-average, seasonal index extraction and normalisation to sum=12. Seasonal index floor at 0.20 with iterative redistribution preserving the sum-to-12 invariant. Dampened seasonality for mixed cost buckets: dampened = 1 + (S-1)(1-fixed_ratio). Why not X-13ARIMA: degrees-of-freedom crisis with 12-24 months of data, non-parametric methods as the honest choice.

Phase 3 — The Accounting Identity: Double-entry bookkeeping as a conservation law (A = L + E preserved by every transaction). P&L projection: revenue × growth × seasonality, costs by CVP type, depreciation, interest, tax. Balance sheet roll-forward: trade receivables (revenue×DSO/7), trade payables (COGS×DPO/7), tax accruals (VAT quarterly drain, PAYE monthly drain, corporation tax annual drain), cash as plug (forces A=L+E). Cash flow statement via indirect method: CFO = net profit + depreciation - ΔAR + ΔAP + Δtax accruals.

Phase 4 — Working Capital Dynamics: DSO (median payment delay, days sales outstanding), DPO (days payable outstanding), CCC = DSO - DPO (cash conversion cycle). Trend detection via OLS on quarterly DSO/DPO. Linear extrapolation with clamping [7, 120]. DSO drag on cash receipts: drag_factor = max(0.70, 1 - annual_drag × year).

Phase 5 — Uncertainty Quantification: Random walks and Brownian motion — variance of sum of independent variables grows as nσ², standard deviation as σ√n (square root of time rule). Uncertainty model σ(w) = min(BASE + k√(w-1), MAX) with CV-informed base rate. Fan charts (symmetric spread = |balance| × σ(w)). Combining independent uncertainties via quadrature σ_total = √(σ₁² + σ₂²). Confidence intervals via z-scores (1.645/1.96/2.576 for 90%/95%/99%).

Phase 6 — Scenarios and Sensitivity: Adjustment algebra — three types (multiplier, delta, override) applied in canonical order. DELAY adjustment for temporal shifts. Fragment application: each fragment applies independently to original baseline (order-independent). Balance cascade: closing(w) = opening(w) + net(w), opening(w+1) = closing(w). Macro impacts: FX exposure, interest rate pass-through, employer NI.

Phase 7 — The Full Model Walkthrough: From ledger to assumptions (11-step derivation of every model parameter from raw accounting data). The 260-week projection engine loop (revenue → costs → P&L → BS roll-forward → CF derivation → verification → uncertainty bands). Week-to-month aggregation (flows sum, stocks snapshot). The 13-week direct method (transaction-level forecasting from invoice/bill due dates). Direct vs indirect method comparison and hybrid handover.

Mathematician biographies: Gauss (least squares), Legendre (priority dispute), Galton (regression to mean, percentiles), Pacioli (double-entry bookkeeping), Brown/Wiener (Brownian motion), Britton/Fisher/Whitley (BoE fan charts), Quetelet (average man), Pearson (standard deviation), Tukey (box plots, IQR), Bernoulli (constant e), Fibonacci (financial mathematics), Horngren (CVP analysis).

Cross-references: Phase 0 statistics used throughout for DSO/DPO (median), regularity testing (CV), trend detection (OLS). Phase 1 compounding powers all projection engines. Phase 2 seasonality multiplies revenue and dampens costs. Phase 3 accounting identity is the constraint enforced at every time step. Phase 4 working capital bridges P&L to BS. Phase 5 uncertainty wraps the point forecast in confidence bands. Phase 6 scenarios modify the baseline. Phase 7 integrates everything.
